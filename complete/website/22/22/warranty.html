Warranty FAQ

Basic warranties typically cover the vehicle's major components including the engine, transmission, drivetrain, and electrical systems. Coverage varies by manufacturer, but most exclude normal wear items like tires, brakes, and fluids.

Manufacturer warranties typically last between 3 to 5 years or 36,000 to 60,000 miles, whichever comes first. Luxury brands often offer longer warranties, sometimes up to 4 years/50,000 miles for bumper-to-bumper coverage and longer for powertrain components.

Most manufacturer warranties are fully transferable to subsequent owners, which can increase the resale value of your vehicle. Some restrictions may apply, so it's best to check with the specific manufacturer for their transfer policy.

An extended warranty, also known as a vehicle service contract, is additional coverage you can purchase to extend your protection beyond the manufacturer's warranty. Whether you need one depends on how long you plan to keep the vehicle, your driving habits, and your tolerance for repair costs.
